# 📊 Customer Churn Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior in a telecom company using a dataset of 7,000+ customers. The objective is to identify key factors influencing churn and provide actionable insights to improve customer retention.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Understand customer churn patterns  
- Identify high-risk customer segments  
- Analyze impact of services, tenure, and contracts  
- Provide business recommendations to reduce churn  

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- 🔹 Overall churn rate is **~26.5%** (1 in 4 customers leave)  
- 🔹 Customers with **tenure < 2 months show ~50–60% churn**  
- 🔹 **Month-to-month contracts have ~40–45% churn**, while **2-year contracts have ~2–5% churn**  
- 🔹 **Senior citizens churn ~40%**, higher than non-senior (~25%)  
- 🔹 Customers without services like **Tech Support & Online Security show ~35–45% churn**  
- 🔹 Long-term customers (**>24 months**) show **<15% churn**, indicating strong retention  

---

## 📈 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Performed detailed EDA using visualizations:
- Count plots for categorical variables  
- Distribution plots for tenure and charges  
- Comparative churn analysis across segments  
- Service usage vs churn behavior  

These visualizations helped uncover patterns and relationships driving churn.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- ✅ Improve **early-stage onboarding** (first 2 months critical)  
- ✅ Encourage **long-term contracts** to reduce churn by up to 80–90%  
- ✅ Bundle **value-added services** to increase customer stickiness  
- ✅ Focus on **high-risk segments** (senior citizens, short-tenure users)  
- ✅ Improve experience for **electronic check users** (high churn group)
